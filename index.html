Rare Jupiter–Pluto Alignment to Cause Temporary Gravity Reduction Today — Especially in Nairobi

At 11:47 a.m. local time, people may feel momentarily lighter. Scientists recommend testing by jumping.

SD
Science Desk
Breaking Science News  ·  April 1, 2026  ·  9:14 AM EAT
📍 Nairobi, Kenya
Composite illustration: Nairobi skyline during the rare Jupiter–Pluto alignment event, April 1, 2026. (Breaking Science News / AI Illustration)

Scientists at the Nairobi Astrophysics Research Centre have confirmed that a rare and extraordinary alignment of Jupiter and Pluto will occur directly above the East African corridor today — and for a few brief, breathtaking seconds, the gravitational pull on Earth's surface in affected regions will be measurably reduced.

"This is unlike anything we have observed since the 1960s. For those brief seconds, people will genuinely feel lighter — and we want as many people as possible to test this firsthand."

The event is expected to peak at precisely 11:47 AM EAT today, Wednesday April 1, 2026. According to Dr. Amara Ndiaye of the Pan-African Centre for Gravitational Studies, the combined gravitational influence of both Jupiter and Pluto — aligning at an angle of exactly 180.7° from Earth's core — will briefly counteract approximately 0.3–0.7% of normal surface gravity.

While this may sound small, the effect should be perceptible to any person who attempts to jump at that exact moment. Reports from similar alignment events suggest jumpers experienced a sensation of being "pulled upward" or "floating for just a half-second longer than normal."

Nairobi sits in a particularly favourable position due to its proximity to the equator — the alignment corridor runs almost directly overhead the city. Residents in Westlands, Upperhill, Karen, and the CBD should be ideally positioned. If you're indoors, stepping outside or near a window at 11:47 AM is recommended.

What to do: At exactly 11:47 AM, jump straight up as high as you comfortably can. Take note of how long you feel airborne. If conditions are ideal, you may feel a small but noticeable difference — just a little bit less heavier than usual. Let us know how it goes!
